My passion for Cybersecurity goes beyond hacking VMs and CTFs. As my knowledge grows due to my curious nature I have decided to share some of that knowledge with you. At the moment, I am getting the following courses ready. It's not an easy task as you may guess! But for now, you can have a look to the materials that will be available to all soon.

They will be all FREE. If you don't want to miss them once I launch them, leave your e-mail below for a notification. No newsletter, no spam, I will just reach you to let you know.

Basic x86_64 Linux Assembly for Hacking

This course will teach you the fundamentals of x86-64 assembly language and its application to cybersecurity. You will learn how to write and debug assembly code, and how to use assembly language to exploit vulnerabilities in software.

C for Cybersecurity

In this course, students will learn about more advanced topics such as pointers, structures, and unions. Students will also learn how to use C to develop common hacking tools, such as port scanners and packet sniffers.

Esoteric Languages I: Malbolge

Malbolge is an esoteric programming language invented by Ben Olmstead in 1998. It was designed to be as difficult to program in as possible, and is often considered to be the most difficult programming language in the world.

Rust 4 Hacking

Rust is becoming increasingly popular for hacking due to its speed, safety, and expressiveness. This course will teach you the fundamentals of Rust programming, with a focus on malware development, cryptography and exploits.

Malware Basics

In this course with hands-on activities, you will be introduced to malware. You will analyze executable files, develop your very own malware and learn how to obfuscate and deliver them as part of your attack.

DIY Hacking Gadgets Course

The first ever course on DIY pentesting gadgets using ESP32, ESP8266, Attiny85, Arduino, and Raspberry Pi... With a focus on making your own gadgets, divided into different projects.
